Rockstar Games has been targeted by hackers again — and the timing couldn't be worse.

Hacking group **ShinyHunters** claims it accessed Rockstar corporate data through a breach of **Anodot**, a third-party analytics platform used by the studio. The group threatened to publish the data if their ransom demands weren't met by April 14. Rockstar confirmed the incident in a statement to Kotaku but said it involved "a limited amount of non-material company information" and that it has "no impact on our organization or our players." The studio confirmed it is not paying the ransom.

Crucially: **no gameplay footage, no GTA 6 source code, and no player data appears to have been compromised.** This is a corporate data breach, not a development leak.

What did leak? The Anodot data reportedly contained internal business analytics — including metrics that revealed GTA Online is still generating extraordinary revenue, which analysts say explains why Take-Two has been comfortable with multiple GTA 6 delays. When your last game is still printing money, you can afford to wait.

GTA 6 remains on track for **November 19, 2026**. Rockstar's official GTA VI page still lists the date unchanged. The real risk here is reputational noise during what should be a positive pre-launch stretch — not actual development impact.

This is far from the studio's first rodeo with hackers. The 2022 breach remains the most significant in gaming history. This one appears considerably more limited in scope.
