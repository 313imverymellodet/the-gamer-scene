Nintendo's new free-to-start competitive battler Pokemon Champions landed on Switch and Switch 2 on April 8, and it hasn't had the smoothest of starts. The game focuses on a global tournament circuit — a sharp departure from the open-world catching and exploring of Scarlet and Violet — but players have been quick to voice frustration over a low Pokemon count at launch, clunky menus, and poor performance even on Switch 2 hardware.

Prominent leaker Centro Leaks called the launch "terrible," and the criticism spread fast enough that the game's developer issued a public apology and committed to fixes in upcoming patches. The apology acknowledges the performance issues specifically and promises an update to address the most pressing complaints.

It's worth noting that Among Us took years to find its audience — Pokemon Champions may yet turn it around — but right now, the competitive Pokemon faithful are not impressed. Keep an eye on patch notes over the coming weeks to see if the developer can right the ship. The bones of an interesting competitive format are there; the execution just isn't there yet.
