# Hades 2 Finally Hits PS5 and Xbox — Console Players Just Got the Best Version of One of the Best Games Ever Made

Seven months. That's how long PlayStation and Xbox players have been pressing their faces against the glass while their PC and Switch counterparts racked up hundreds of hours in Hades 2 and shared increasingly unhinged build screenshots in every gaming Discord in existence. The wait is over. As of April 14, 2026, **Hades 2 is available on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X|S** — and not only is it worth every second of the wait, the console versions are arguably the definitive way to experience the game.

## What is Hades 2?

If you somehow avoided all coverage of Supergiant Games' sequel since its Early Access launch in 2024: Hades 2 is a **roguelite action game** set in Greek mythology, developed by Supergiant Games, the studio responsible for Bastion, Transistor, Pyre, and the original Hades. You play as **Melinoë**, daughter of Hades and Princess of the Underworld, who has been raised in secret by the witch Hecate while her entire family — including her brother Zagreus, the protagonist of the first game — has been captured by **Chronos, the Titan of Time**.

Your job is to fight your way through the procedurally assembled layers of the Underworld, die repeatedly, return stronger each time, and eventually reach Chronos. That basic loop — run, die, grow, try again — is the backbone of every roguelite ever made. What Hades 2 does with that backbone is something else entirely.

Each run is populated with encounters and characters from across Greek mythology, many of them less familiar than the original game's cast. Arachne, Scylla, Narcissus, and a take on the gods of Olympus that is distinctly Supergiant's own all appear throughout the campaign. Every significant character has voiced dialogue that evolves across dozens of conversations, and the script is massive — the game logs hundreds of distinct voice lines per major character. The narrative feels alive in a way that most story-driven games with fixed dialogue trees can't match, because Hades 2's story is built to unfold organically across thirty, forty, a hundred hours of play.

## The Mechanical Heart

The combat builds on Hades 1's foundation with more weapons, more Olympian boon options, and a new layer of build complexity that rewards experimentation. Melinoë fights differently to Zagreus — her moves have a different cadence, and the new **Hex** abilities, charged through combat, add a secondary resource to manage that dramatically expands the strategic ceiling. The **Arcana card system** provides permanent passive upgrades to equip before each run. The cauldron in the hub world lets you unlock new features and zones through incantations that require specific resources gathered during runs. The systems interlock elegantly, and the feeling of gradually understanding how everything fits together is one of the great joys of the first twenty hours.

A new **God Mode** option, tuned since Early Access based on community feedback, provides accessibility without breaking the experience — the damage reduction scales dynamically based on how you're doing, meaning it genuinely adapts to where players are struggling rather than just flattening the whole game.

## Why the Console Versions Are the Definitive Edition

Here's what PlayStation and Xbox players get on day one that PC players didn't have until months into Early Access:

**Patch 2 baked in.** The April 2026 update — which deploys simultaneously across all platforms — is the most substantial balance and content pass Supergiant has released since 1.0. Console players launch with the fully refined, best-balanced version of the game. PC players in Early Access played through multiple iterations to get here. Console players start at the destination.

**120fps.** On PS5 and Xbox Series X, Hades 2 targets 120 frames per second and delivers it. For a game where split-second dodges and reaction windows define success or failure, the input responsiveness at 120fps is genuinely meaningful. Supergiant's Greg Kasavin confirmed that the PS5 version is already at full quality, meaning there are no PS5 Pro enhancements — the base PS5 is running the game exactly as intended.

**DualSense integration.** The PS5 version uses haptic feedback and adaptive triggers to give each weapon a distinct physical feel. This is the kind of feature that sounds marginal in a spec sheet and feels meaningful in practice. The Moonstone Axe and the Argent Skull, in particular, feel different in the hands in ways that deepen engagement with the weapon variety.

**Day-one Game Pass.** Xbox players get Hades 2 included in Game Pass Ultimate and PC Game Pass from launch. At £24.99 to buy outright, that's already exceptional value — as a Game Pass inclusion it's one of the strongest additions the service has had this year.

**Xbox Play Anywhere.** The Xbox version syncs progress between Xbox Series X|S and Windows PC through the Microsoft Store. If you play on both, your save follows you.

The main caveat: **there is no cross-save between PS5/Xbox and Switch or PC.** Switch and PC players share cross-save between each other, but if you put 200 hours into the Switch 2 version, you're starting fresh on PS5. That stings for some, but for anyone coming in new, it doesn't matter at all.

## The Critical Consensus

RPGSite, one of the most thorough reviewers of the PC version, gave Hades 2 a **10/10**, calling it "one of my favorite games of 2025." GameSpew gave it **5 out of 5** for the PS5 build, describing it as "the gold standard of roguelike action games." The ResetEra review thread is filled with first-time players having the exact reaction veteran players remember from their own first encounters with the game: "I finally understand why everyone won't shut up about this."

## Should You Play It?

If you've never played Hades or Hades 2 and you own a PS5 or Xbox, this is an easy decision. Start playing. If you've already completed Hades 2 on PC or Switch, the console builds don't add enough new content to justify a double-dip unless you specifically want the DualSense experience or the convenience of console play. If you have Xbox Game Pass and haven't played it yet, there is genuinely no reason to delay — it is free, it is on your console, and it is exceptional.

**Hades 2 is available now on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, Switch, Switch 2, and PC. $24.99 / Free on Xbox Game Pass.**
