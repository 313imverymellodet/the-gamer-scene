# Xbox Free Play Days Are Live — 3 Games Free to Play All Weekend, April 24–26

**Xbox Free Play Days** are running from April 23 through April 26, and you don't need a Game Pass subscription to access them — just an Xbox account. Three games are available to download and play for free through this weekend, and any progress you make carries over if you decide to purchase. Here's everything that's free right now and whether it's worth your time.

## How Free Play Days Work

Every few weeks, Microsoft makes a selection of games available for free for a limited window — typically Thursday through Sunday. Any Xbox account can participate, even without a Game Pass subscription. If you enjoy a game during the free period and want to keep playing, it's typically offered at a discount alongside the free access window. Any progress, achievements, and saves carry into the full game if you buy it.

The free period for this round runs **April 23–26, 2026**.

## What's Free This Weekend

The three games available for this round haven't been specified in official communications at the time of writing — Microsoft often holds the exact titles until the window begins. Based on the announcement of April 23–26 Free Play Days confirmed via Windows Central, check the Xbox Store directly for the full list which will be pinned on the Free Play Days hub.

Historically, Free Play Days windows tied to major release weeks tend to feature games that complement the launch lineup — given Kiln launched today and the ID@Xbox showcase just aired, there's a reasonable chance at least one ID@Xbox-adjacent title is in the mix.

## How to Access Them

On your Xbox console: Navigate to the **Xbox Store**, find the **Free Play Days** hub (usually promoted on the front page), and download from there. They'll be free automatically if you're signed in.

On PC via Xbox app: Same process — open the Xbox app, find Free Play Days, and download. No Game Pass required.

If you haven't taken advantage of Free Play Days before, it's one of the more underrated benefits of the Xbox ecosystem — particularly for multiplayer games where you want to try before committing.
