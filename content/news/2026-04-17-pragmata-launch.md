Capcom's long-gestating sci-fi action game Pragmata launched April 17 on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC, and Switch 2, delivering on years of promise with a unique hacking twist that critics say elevates it above a standard third-person shooter. The game follows Hugh, a space auditor stranded on a lunar research station, and his android companion Diana, as they navigate malfunctioning AI systems in a near-future thriller that blends action with puzzle-based hacking mini-games.

PC Gamer scored it 87%, calling it "a snazzy late aughts throwback elevated by a terrific sense of feedback and momentum." The demo alone racked up 2 million downloads, matching its wishlists, signalling demand was very real. Steam users are currently rating it 97% positive — a remarkable result for a game many had written off as vaporware.

The RE Engine continues to do incredible work. Pragmata's corrupted recreation of New York — deliberately glitched out and subtly wrong in ways the developers intentionally baked in — is one of the most striking environments of the year. Frame rates held rock solid across PS5 and PC, and the Switch 2 version holds up better than anyone had a right to expect given the scale of the game.

If you were waiting on reviews before pulling the trigger, the verdict is in: this one delivers.
