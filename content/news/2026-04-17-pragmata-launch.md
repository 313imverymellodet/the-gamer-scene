After six years of cryptic trailers and delay after delay, **Pragmata** has landed on PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC, and Switch 2 — and it delivered on every bit of the hype.

Capcom's long-gestating sci-fi action game follows Hugh, a space auditor stranded on a malfunctioning lunar research station, and Diana, his android companion who can hack enemy systems in real time. The two-character dynamic is the game's beating heart, and it works. The hacking-plus-shooting combat system is genuinely fresh — PC Gamer scored it 87%, calling it "a snazzy late aughts throwback elevated by a terrific sense of feedback and momentum."

The numbers back it up. The demo alone racked up 2 million downloads before launch, and the full game is currently sitting at 97% positive on Steam from nearly 7,000 user reviews. On Metacritic, scores range from 85–88 depending on platform, making it one of the highest-rated games of 2026 so far — behind only Capcom's own Resident Evil Requiem.

This is Capcom's third major critical hit of the year, following Resident Evil Requiem and Monster Hunter Stories 3. It's becoming genuinely hard to argue against them being the best publisher in games right now.

**Pragmata is available now. $59.99 on all platforms.**
