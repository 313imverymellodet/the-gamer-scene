Monday means it's time to look ahead. This week is stacked — here's your full breakdown.

---

### April 21 — Vampire Crawlers
**Platforms:** PS5, Xbox, Switch, PC | **Price:** $9.99 | **Game Pass:** Day One

The most anticipated indie release of the month. Developer Poncle — creators of Vampire Survivors — spin off into an entirely new genre with this hyper turn-based deckbuilding dungeon crawler. You'll build card decks, explore familiar VS locations in first-person blobber style, and unleash broken combos using the new Turboturn System. The demo earned a near-perfect reception during Steam Next Fest and demo saves carry over to the full game. At $9.99 (and free on Game Pass), this is a no-brainer.

---

### April 21 — WoW Midnight Patch 12.0.5
**Platform:** PC | **Game:** World of Warcraft: Midnight

Midnight's first post-launch content update arrives tomorrow and it's bringing a lot. **Void Assaults** are large rotating zone events in Eversong Woods and Zul'Aman. **The Voidforge** is a new gear upgrade system for chasing best-in-slot equipment. **Ritual Sites** are small 1–5 player scenarios. And **Decor Duels** is a hide-and-seek PvP mode in Silvermoon City where players disguise themselves as housing furniture. Also: housing UI improvements, new transmog options, and a Twitch Drop event for the Cuddly Pearl Grrgle decor item starting April 23.

---

### April 22 — Masters of Albion
**Platform:** PC Only | **Dev:** Peter Molyneux / Lionhead veterans

Fable creator Peter Molyneux returns with a god-game city builder set in Albion. Shape an entire living world — build towns, guide (or torment) your citizens, and play deity across a real-time strategy and simulation sandbox. Molyneux has called it his chance at redemption after years of over-promising. PC exclusive at launch.

---

### April 22 — Tides of Tomorrow
**Platforms:** PS5, Xbox, PC | **Dev:** Digixart (Road 96)

A "plastic punk" narrative adventure set in a flooded world. The signature feature is the **Online Story-Link system** — your choices alter other players' worlds and vice versa, creating a shared narrative experience. From the makers of Road 96.

---

### April 23 — Kiln
**Platforms:** PS5, Xbox, PC | **Game Pass:** Day One | **Dev:** Double Fine Productions

Double Fine's new game is a competitive online pottery brawler. Sculpt ceramic battle armour on a pottery wheel, then take it into PvP arenas to smash your opponents' creations. It sounds absurd and it looks incredible. Day one on Game Pass.

---

**Also on the horizon this week:** Little Nightmares VR: Altered Echoes drops April 24 for PS5, PSVR2, and Quest. Full standalone VR entry in the franchise.
