# This Week in Games: Vampire Crawlers, WoW Midnight Patch 12.0.5, Masters of Albion, Kiln, and More

April doesn't slow down. After Hades 2 hitting consoles, Pragmata's launch, Mouse: P.I. For Hire, and the Battlefield 6 roadmap drop all crammed into one week, you'd be forgiven for expecting things to ease up as the month heads into its final stretch. Instead, this week brings a Vampire Survivors spin-off, a major WoW expansion patch, a Peter Molyneux comeback, a narrative adventure about the end of the world, and a pottery brawler from Double Fine. Buckle up.

---

## Tuesday, April 21 — Vampire Crawlers

**Platforms:** PS5, PS4, Xbox Series X|S, Xbox One, Switch, Switch 2, PC
**Price:** $9.99
**Xbox Game Pass:** Day One

The most anticipated indie drop of the month, and it's arriving tomorrow. **Vampire Crawlers** is the first major spin-off from developer **Poncle**, the one-person studio behind Vampire Survivors — the 2022 bullet-heaven indie that sold millions of copies, won a BAFTA, and inspired an entire subgenre of clones in the years since.

Vampire Crawlers takes everything you love about the Vampire Survivors universe — the characters, the locations, the escalating chaos — and transposes it into an entirely different genre. This is a **turn-based, deckbuilding, first-person dungeon crawler**, often called a "blobber" in reference to the classic dungeon-crawling games of the late 80s and early 90s. You explore familiar VS dungeons from a first-person perspective, navigate walls and traps, and fight enemies by building and playing card decks rather than surviving overwhelming hordes.

The central mechanical invention is the **Turboturn System**, which allows players to either take their time with tactical deliberation or play turns as fast as they physically can — with the game promising that both approaches produce accurate, intentional results. It's a design philosophy that suggests Poncle wants the game to work equally well for careful strategists and chaotic speedrunners.

The demo, available throughout Steam Next Fest and on Xbox, was genuinely excellent. Players who played it are reporting dozens of hours already — with a common thread being that the demo builds carry over to the full game, meaning anyone who enjoyed the preview content starts with meaningful progress. The community reaction has been overwhelmingly positive, with ResetEra threads full of players saying they can't stop thinking about the card combinations they want to try in the full release.

At **$9.99** — roughly double Vampire Survivors' original launch price but still extraordinarily cheap for what appears to be a content-rich full game — and **day one on Xbox Game Pass**, this is about as low-risk a purchase as it gets. Whether you're a Vampire Survivors veteran or approaching Poncle's universe for the first time, Vampire Crawlers looks like exactly the kind of "accidentally no-lifed it for two weeks" game that only comes around a few times a year.

## Tuesday, April 21 — World of Warcraft Midnight: Patch 12.0.5

**Platform:** PC
**Game:** World of Warcraft: Midnight

WoW's Midnight expansion is only a few months old and already Blizzard is pushing its first substantial post-launch content update. Patch 12.0.5 drops tomorrow, and it's bringing enough new material to re-energise the player base heading into May.

The headliner is **Void Assaults** — large-scale, zone-wide events in Eversong Woods and Zul'Aman that trigger on a weekly rotation. Once enough smaller Void Strikes have been completed in a zone, a full Void Assault kicks off, requiring coordinated effort from many players to defeat. The design echoes older WoW open-world events but has been updated for Midnight's more contemporary pacing.

**The Voidforge** is the gear upgrade system introduced in 12.0.5, built around Nebulous Voidcores earned through end-game content — Season 1 raids, Mythic+ dungeons, Bountiful Delves, and Nightmare difficulty Prey Hunts. The Voidforge lets players target-craft and upgrade specific pieces, giving dedicated end-gamers a path to chase best-in-slot items with less reliance on pure RNG. Later in the patch, the system expands to the Ascendant Nilhammer, which upgrades weapons and trinkets to new maximum item levels using Ascendant Voidcores.

**Ritual Sites** are small one-to-five player scenarios scatted across the Midnight zones, targeting cultists and naga who are attempting to empower themselves through dark rituals. Accessible solo or in small groups, these give players a reason to be out in the world outside of organised group content.

The wildcard addition is **Decor Duels** — a team-versus-team hide-and-seek PvP mode taking place entirely in Silvermoon City, accessed through the Group Finder's PvP tab. One team disguises themselves as housing decor items. The other team finds them. It's chaotic, it earns rewards including mounts and toys, and it is exactly the kind of weird, playful content Blizzard adds to WoW when they're feeling confident. It will either become a beloved mini-game or be forgotten within a month. Probably the former.

Patch 12.0.5 also includes housing UI updates (furniture can now be linked in chat), new transmog Situations (context-specific outfit rules, including one for when it's raining), and a Twitch Drop event starting **April 23** that rewards the Cuddly Pearl Grrgle housing decor item for watching any WoW stream for four hours. The drop runs until May 21.

## Wednesday, April 22 — Masters of Albion

**Platform:** PC Only (at launch)
**Developer:** Peter Molyneux / Lionhead veterans

**Peter Molyneux** is back. The creator of Fable, Black & White, Dungeon Keeper, and Populous — one of the most influential and simultaneously controversial figures in game development history — returns this week with **Masters of Albion**, a god-game city builder set in a living world that players shape from scratch.

Molyneux has described Masters of Albion as his chance at redemption. He's been open in interviews about the gap between the ambitions he's articulated over the years and the games those ambitions produced — the notorious Fable 1 promises, the divisive nature of Fable 2 and 3, and the troubled mobile projects that followed. Masters of Albion is built on the foundation of classics like Black & White and Dungeon Keeper but updated for modern sensibilities and developed with a team of veterans who worked on those original titles.

The game puts players in the role of a deity overseeing a newly created world populated with people who will react to every decision you make. Build towns. Manage resources. Guide your population toward a flourishing civilisation. Or, if you prefer, torment them. Shape the geography. Respond to crises. Watch the world evolve in real time based on the choices you make and the kind of god you choose to be. The studio has emphasised that "there is no single solution" — the game is designed to support wildly different approaches to the same scenarios.

PC exclusive at launch. The development team has described a phased platform strategy but haven't committed to console dates yet.

## Wednesday, April 22 — Tides of Tomorrow

**Platforms:** PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC
**Developer:** Digixart (Road 96)

**Tides of Tomorrow** is the new narrative adventure from **Digixart**, the studio behind Road 96 — the acclaimed 2021 game about hitchhiking across a country on the verge of revolution. If you've played Road 96, you know what Digixart does well: character-driven, choice-heavy storytelling where the emotional weight of small decisions compounds across a long journey.

Tides of Tomorrow takes that philosophy and places it in a **"plastic punk" future** where rising sea levels and environmental collapse have left the world's surviving populations clinging to what remains. Players navigate this flooded world making choices that don't just affect their own story — the game's headline feature is the **Online Story-Link system**, which connects individual playthroughs in a shared narrative web. Choices you make during your game ripple outward and alter the experiences of other players online. Their choices affect yours. The world is collaborative even when you're playing alone.

It's an ambitious concept that recalls Death Stranding's approach to asynchronous player connection — contribution and consequence shared across a community of strangers who never directly interact. Whether Digixart executes it as cleanly as the concept deserves will be the central question when reviews arrive.

## Thursday, April 23 — Kiln

**Platforms:** PS5, Xbox Series X|S, PC
**Developer:** Double Fine Productions
**Xbox Game Pass:** Day One

You sculpt a pot. You bring the pot into battle. You smash your opponents' pots. Their pots become the bodies they fight with, and your pot is your body. Win by destroying the pots of everyone who challenges you.

**Kiln** is Double Fine Productions' new game, and it is exactly as wonderfully deranged as it sounds.

Double Fine — the studio founded by Tim Schafer and known for Psychonauts, Brütal Legend, and a catalogue of beloved creative swings — describes Kiln as an "online multiplayer pottery power-fantasy celebrating both creativity and destruction." The pottery wheel serves as both game mechanic and metaphor: players use it to sculpt customised ceramic forms that become their player characters, meaning the shape and structure of your pot — how thick the walls are, where the mass is distributed, how you've decorated it — has implications for how you fight.

The PvP component is framed around arena battles in which ceramic creations are smashed against one another with the explicit goal of producing the most satisfying possible moment of destruction. Seasonal content, cosmetics, and progression systems wrap around the core loop. It's available **day one on Xbox Game Pass**, which is the most sensible delivery vehicle for a game that the entire world needs to try but that nobody can necessarily justify paying full price for based on a description that sounds like a fever dream.
