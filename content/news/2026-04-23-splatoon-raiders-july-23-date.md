# Splatoon Raiders Gets a Release Date — Nintendo's First Splatoon Spinoff Hits Switch 2 on July 23

In the most Nintendo way possible — a surprise drop on the Nintendo Today app with no warning on a random Tuesday morning — **Splatoon Raiders** received its release date this week: **July 23, 2026**, exclusively on Nintendo Switch 2. The announcement came with a new trailer showcasing gameplay, a weapon upgrade system, player customization options, and the Salmonid enemies that will populate the mysterious **Spirhalite Islands**.

It's been almost a year since Splatoon Raiders was first announced. The wait is nearly over.

## What Is Splatoon Raiders?

Splatoon Raiders is Splatoon's first dedicated spinoff — and a significant departure from the franchise's multiplayer-first identity. This is primarily a **single-player action game** with optional co-op for up to four players, either online or locally. You play as a mechanic character you create and customize, teaming up with **Deep Cut** — the trio of Shiver, Frye, and Big Man introduced in Splatoon 3 — to raid the Spirhalite Islands for treasure.

The premise is built around exploration, combat, and loot extraction from hostile environments. Salmonid enemies, familiar to Splatoon 3 players from its Salmon Run mode, populate the islands — but the new trailer confirmed both familiar and brand-new Salmonid types that players haven't encountered before. The gameplay loop centres on equipping ink-splattering weapons and mechanical gadgets, defeating enemies, and extracting loot from each area.

The trailer showed **Big Man operating a manta-ray-powered jet-ski**, which is the most Splatoon sentence ever written, and confirmed that the weapon upgrade system lets players develop their loadout over the course of the game rather than selecting from a fixed roster.

## Pricing

$50 digital, $60 physical. The slightly lower-than-standard digital price is a mild surprise — Nintendo has been pricing Switch 2 titles at $60 standard digitally for most major releases.

## The Switch 2 Summer Lineup Takes Shape

The release date confirmation comes at a moment when Nintendo's 2026 summer lineup is crystallising into something genuinely impressive for Switch 2 owners. The current confirmed calendar:

- **May:** Yoshi and the Mysterious Book
- **July 2:** Rhythm Heaven Groove
- **July 9:** Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced (multiplatform)
- **July 23:** Splatoon Raiders
- **2026:** The Duskbloods (FromSoftware), Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave, rumoured Star Fox

Three Nintendo first-party or second-party releases in roughly two months of summer. For a platform in its first full year, the content cadence is strong — and the Splatoon franchise has consistently proven to be one of Nintendo's most reliable sellers, particularly among younger audiences and competitive players.

## Amiibo Confirmed

Three new **Deep Cut amiibo** — jumbo-sized redesigns of Shiver, Frye, and Big Man — are releasing alongside the game. Pricing hasn't been announced, and what they unlock in Splatoon Raiders specifically hasn't been confirmed, but cosmetic bonuses or weapon unlocks are the most likely application given franchise precedent.

## Why This Matters Beyond Splatoon

The broader significance of Splatoon Raiders is what it represents for the franchise. Every previous Splatoon entry has been built around competitive multiplayer as its primary offering. Raiders is the first time Nintendo is testing a fully story-driven, exploration-based Splatoon experience as a standalone product. If it succeeds — and the IP has more than enough goodwill to give it a strong launch — it establishes that Splatoon can sustain a diversified game library beyond the numbered sequels.

That's meaningful for a franchise that, per Nintendo's own data, has some of the most dedicated platform fans in gaming.

**Splatoon Raiders launches July 23, 2026 exclusively on Nintendo Switch 2. $50 digital / $60 physical.**
