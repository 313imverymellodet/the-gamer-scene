What happens when you throw a boomer shooter into a blender with BioShock influences and a 1930s rubber-hose cartoon aesthetic? You get Mouse: P.I. For Hire, the stylish black-and-white first-person shooter that launched April 16 on PS5, Xbox, PC, Switch, and Switch 2.

Players take on the role of Jack Pepper, a gumshoe detective navigating a world of noir storytelling, exaggerated cartoon violence, and dark humour — all rendered in hand-drawn 2D animation that makes it look unlike anything else on the market this year. Enemies squash and stretch. Bullets are drawn in thick ink. Explosions bloom like comic panel splash pages. It is, genuinely, extraordinary to look at.

GameSpot described it as "cute — but violent!" and praised its distinctive visual identity. Destructoid handed it a 9.5/10, calling it "a true passion project that's pure entertainment from start to finish." If you want something completely different from the usual grey-brown military shooter, this is your game. A demo is also available on most platforms if you want to try before you buy at $39.99.
