Blizzard has been busy preparing Diablo 4 for its upcoming Lord of Hatred expansion, due April 28 on PS4, PS5, Xbox, and PC. Ahead of launch, the studio revealed a lengthy list of changes coming with Patch 12.0.5 on April 21, signalling a big week of Blizzard updates across all its titles. Mark your calendars for April 23 as well — Blizzard has a special event planned that fans of the expansion will want to tune into.

The expansion is shaping up to be one of the biggest content drops in Diablo 4's lifecycle, and comes at a crucial moment for Blizzard, which has been working to re-energize its action RPG against stiff competition from Path of Exile 2. Early indications suggest Lord of Hatred will bring a new act, fresh endgame systems, and additional classes — though the studio is keeping some details under wraps until closer to release.

If you haven't touched Diablo 4 in a while, now is a great time to jump back in and get your character ready. The April 28 launch is ten days away — and based on what Blizzard has teased so far, this one looks like it could be a genuine turning point for the game's long-term health.
