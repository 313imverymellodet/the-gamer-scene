# Forza Horizon 6 Sets Course for Japan — Everything You Need to Know Before May 19

The next entry in the Forza Horizon series arrives in less than a month, and it's making history: **Forza Horizon 6** is set in **Japan**, marking the first time the franchise has taken its open-world racing festival to the country. The official release date is **May 19, 2026**, with Premium Edition early access beginning on **May 15** — four days earlier for players willing to pay the premium entry price.

Here is everything you need to know before launch.

## Why Japan Was Always the Right Call

Forza Horizon has cycled through the UK, Southern Europe, Australia, the American Southwest, Mexico, and various fictional blends over its lifespan. Japan has been the most consistently requested setting in the community for years — and for obvious reasons. The country offers a uniquely diverse landscape for an open-world racer: dense urban environments with signature neon and expressway culture, mountain touge roads, rural coastal highways, seasonal variety that no other potential setting can match, and a motorsport heritage that includes some of the most iconic cars ever produced.

Playground Games confirmed the Japan setting during the Xbox Developer_Direct earlier this year, and since then the community has been dissecting every screenshot and trailer frame for location clues. The confirmed presence of urban cityscapes, mountain driving sections, and what appear to be coastal routes suggests the game is being built to use the country's geographical diversity rather than just its iconic aesthetic.

## Editions and Pricing

**Standard Edition:** Free on Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, PC Game Pass, and Xbox Game Pass on day one. Purchasing outright is available for those without a subscription.

**Premium Edition:** $129.99. Includes four days of early access (May 15 launch), the full game, an expansion pass covering two post-launch expansions, and a VIP membership with in-game bonuses.

The Game Pass inclusion is significant. Forza Horizon 5 demonstrated that including the flagship Xbox racing game in the subscription service drives massive concurrent player numbers and extends the community's lifecycle. Horizon 6 landing in Game Pass from day one means effectively every Game Pass subscriber has access on May 19 — which, given the current strength of the Game Pass library, is a meaningful part of Microsoft's spring gaming strategy.

## What We Know About the Game

**Platform confirmation:** Xbox Series X|S, PC, and Xbox Cloud Gaming. A PS5 version has been confirmed for "later in 2026" — Playground Games and Microsoft have confirmed the game will eventually come to PlayStation, continuing the cross-platform expansion strategy that brought Halo and other former Xbox exclusives to PS5.

**Japan setting specifics:** The confirmed landscape includes urban driving environments, mountain passes (an obvious home for the franchise's traditional point-to-point races), and rural coastal roads. Cherry blossom seasonal events have been referenced in pre-release coverage, suggesting dynamic seasonal progression continues from previous games.

**Returning features:** The Horizon Festival structure, Auction House, Forza Link social features, and the Horizon Arcade multiplayer events are all confirmed to return. Specific new features haven't been detailed ahead of launch, which suggests Playground is saving reveals for the final pre-launch marketing push.

**The car list:** A complete official list hasn't been confirmed, but a Japanese setting predictably opens doors for specific manufacturer focus — Honda, Toyota, Nissan, Subaru, Mazda, and Mitsubishi all have iconic cars with strong Forza Horizon histories. The presence of Japanese domestic market legends in the lineup seems certain.

## The Game Pass Value Case

Let's be direct: if you have Xbox Game Pass Ultimate, Forza Horizon 6 costs you nothing additional on May 19. That's a game that, purchased outright, would likely cost $69.99. The value maths are straightforward, and Horizon games have consistently been among the most content-rich open-world games in any genre. Forza Horizon 5 accumulated over two years of updates and is still an active, thriving community experience years after launch. Horizon 6 has every reason to follow the same trajectory.

For PlayStation players: a PS5 version is coming "later in 2026," but no specific date has been given. If Japan-set open-world racing is something you want to experience immediately, this is the moment to consider whether Game Pass is worth the entry cost.

## What's Coming After Launch

The Premium Edition expansion pass covers two post-launch expansions, continuing Horizon's tradition of substantial story-driven DLC experiences. Specific expansion details haven't been announced.

The spring and summer showcase season — Xbox's showcase is June 7 — will likely provide the first detailed look at post-launch content plans alongside the expected Starfield Switch 2 and other platform announcements.

**Forza Horizon 6 launches May 19, 2026 on Xbox Series X|S, PC, and Xbox Cloud. Free on Game Pass. Premium Edition early access May 15 for $129.99. PS5 version later in 2026.**
