What happens when you throw a boomer shooter into a blender with BioShock influences and a 1930s rubber-hose cartoon? You get **Mouse: P.I. For Hire**, the stylish black-and-white first-person shooter that launched April 16 from debut developer Fumi Games.

Players take on the role of **Jack Pepper**, a mouse detective voiced by **Troy Baker**, investigating corruption in the city of Mouseburg. The arsenal reads like a 1930s crime prop list — revolvers, tommy guns, Carcano rifles, and dynamite — and the **Fantastic-o-Matic** vending machine power-up system adds replayability to every level. The entire game is rendered in hand-drawn 2D rubber-hose animation, the same style as early Mickey Mouse cartoons, and it is genuinely jaw-dropping.

Critics have responded strongly. Destructoid scored it **9.5/10**, Console Creatures **9/10**, and it currently sits at **81 on Metacritic** and **83 on OpenCritic** — impressive numbers for a debut title. The main criticism across reviews is that the detective narrative elements feel underdeveloped compared to the depth of the combat. The final section also drags. But the consensus is clear: this is one of the best indie shooters in years.

It launched the same day as Pragmata and still carved out its own conversation. At **$39.99**, it's an easy recommendation for anyone who wants something completely different from the usual AAA noise.

**Mouse: P.I. For Hire is available now on PS5, Xbox, Switch, and PC.**
