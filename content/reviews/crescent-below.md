Neon Sigil's debut is a game of extraordinary moments and frustrating inconsistency. The first four hours are as good as anything released this year — a fever dream of environmental storytelling and razor-sharp movement design. Then the seams start to show.

The ending, in particular, feels like a different team finished it under deadline pressure. What begins as a singular vision frays into something generic. Still worth playing for what it gets right.

**Verdict:** Recommended with caveats. Play for the highs; forgive the lows.
