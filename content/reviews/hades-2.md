Writing a Hades II review in April 2026 feels a little absurd. PC and Switch players have had it since September — the internet has been full of takes, guides, and fan art for months. So let's frame this as what it actually is: a verdict for the PlayStation 5 and Xbox players who waited, and a question about whether the wait was worth it.

The short answer is an unambiguous yes. The longer answer involves talking about Melinoë, daughter of Hades, who has arguably surpassed her brother Zagreus as the most compelling protagonist Supergiant has ever written. Her mission — descend into an Underworld now controlled by Chronos, the Titan of Time, to save her captured family including Zagreus himself — carries higher mythological stakes than the original, and Supergiant fully commits to the scale of it.

For the uninitiated: Hades II is a roguelite action game where you run through procedurally assembled gauntlets, die, and try again with a growing arsenal of permanent upgrades and Olympian blessings. That loop was already perfect in the first game. Here it's deeper, wider, and more rewarding — a new cauldron system for permanent unlocks, an Arcana card system for run-building, a hub world that meaningfully evolves as you progress, and a boss roster that PC players have been arguing about for months.

On PS5, the game is a visual revelation on a big screen. The inky, hand-painted art direction — all layered silhouettes and glowing divine light — pops in a way that the Switch 2 handheld screen simply couldn't capture. The game targets 120fps and largely delivers it without compromise. No performance mode vs graphics mode toggle required — just clean, fluid, beautiful action at all times. DualSense haptics add satisfying weight to each weapon swing.

Xbox players get the best value in the industry right now: Hades II is day-one on Game Pass, plays perfectly on Series X at 120fps, and supports Xbox Play Anywhere, meaning your save carries between console and PC. If you have a subscription and haven't downloaded it yet, you're leaving money on the table.

Our only real hesitation in giving this a perfect score is purely platform-specific: there's no cross-save between PS5/Xbox and Switch or PC, which stings for players who put hours in elsewhere. But taken as a complete experience — and for anyone coming in fresh — Hades II on PS5 and Xbox isn't just the best way to play. It's one of the finest games this generation has produced.
