Paradox has made a sequel that assumes you've done your homework. Field Marshal 2 doesn't hold your hand, doesn't explain its systems in exhaustive tutorials, and doesn't apologize for its complexity. It just trusts you to figure it out — and that trust is enormously refreshing.

Forty-eight hours in, I'm still discovering new interactions between systems. The AI opponents are the best in the series. The campaign is merciless. I've rescheduled three social engagements to keep playing it.

**Verdict:** Strategy fans, this is your game of the year.
